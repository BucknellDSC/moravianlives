VIII The Matrone Susan KrishkerThe following is a Account of herself: I am born 1712 the 29th of Sept. at Hirsground in Upper Silesie. I was but one Year old when my Vater departed Life. In my 6th Year of Age I was inticed drawnat once in my Heart to go in a Corner & there to fall down & pray to dear God, which I accordingly did. As soon my Mother & Sisters perceived my doing so (because I did it often) they was very hard to me because they was afeareth that I should become to melancholy & may be quite BraincrakedBraincracked. Therefore I seek for such PoeplePeople, which could tell me what I wanted & did't know myself. This was soon 1

bestowed on me, then I came to Langendorf & found some People which meet together, reading Books and talking together about spiritual Matters. This I lickedliked very well but for all that it would't do me Satisfaction. A.o1740 I came to Hillersdorf & heard there the first Time of our dear Saviour, this touched my Heart, & from that time I found no more Rest till I resolved to move to the Congregation; but a soon my Mother get a Hint of this my Resolution, she was very angry & sent me to her Sister, which was very watchfull upon me. However our Sav.r heard my continual Prayer & directed it also: As B.r & Sister Beutel pasd this Way, they promissedpromisedupon my Desire, to take me along & I soon was ready and travelled w.th them & our Saviour guided us save to Gnadenfry. O how thankfullthankful was my Heart that I at last found what I so long desireth. Here I lerntlearned my Heart deeper to know, but by that, I found the Heart of my Sav.rallwaysalwaysgratiousgracious & full of Mercy, & that made me pour out many a Tears in his Heart. A. 44 the 24th of May I was a Pertaker of the holy Sacrament & the following November I have been receivd w.th great Number into the Congregation, As there was no Reception a long While, a good many Breth.r & Sisters which was gone since to our Lords Supper was among them. A.o 46 in the Month of July I became very sick, therefore they took me into the Choir House

for better to attent one. A.o57 I became Choir Disciple which brought many a new Blessings about my Heart, and from a much more intime Conversation arose w.th my truest Friend I could in a very familiar Way converse w.th my Saviour as one Friend w.th the Other.

So far She herself. This Testimony we can give her: that She was a true and faithfullfaithful Heart. The 11th of Febr. She was taken sick, as She came into the Sick Room She declared herself also: God be blessed! now I'll lay down my Doings & never think to grow well again, I'll employ my Sabbath Days to the Interest of my Soul; & I can be easy I have no DisatisfactionDissatisfaction, nothing against nobody. She was a pleasant & happy sick Person, her Heart delighted in his Passion & sometimes talkd conversd w.th our Saviour in the so tender intim & submisse a ManerManner, that the other Sick Sisters melted in Tears. As her Labourer came to see & speak w.th her, She said: Our Saviour was so very gratiousgracious to me in my Health & now I am sick he made it so easy to me, that I scarcely feel the Pain of which other sick Persons so much complain of. O there are many Things for which I have my deasrest Sav.r1000 times to praissepraise, & 1000 ThancksThanks are not 2 enough for all what he has done on my poor Soul. She repeated very often: O my dearest Lord, nothing but thine Blood & SuffringSuffering & Wounds is it, which has taken & captivated my Heart: and so it shall remain for ever. This Morning the 23th She declard: how happy am I! Myn Sabbaths Day is at Hand, the greatest Day I ever knew. At Noon, her expected & happy Hour struck at which the Blessing of her Choir was bestowed on her, & so her Mouth grow pale in her Soul went home to her Friend in the 51st Year of her Age.
